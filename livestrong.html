Pursuing an Opportunity in the Web Fitness Space

At a Glance

Livestrong.com is a licensee of the Livestrong Organization's brand. We focus on content that helps readers get fit, eat better, manage weight, and overall - live well. As part of this particular project, our team reimagined what the fitness content web experience could be.

User Goal

Learn about the right ways to perform and execute individual exercises and comprehensive routines.

Business Goal

Position the Brand as a Leading Resource in Fitness Content

Role

Product Designer

Outcome

MVP was a success. The template became a core feature and gained more resource allocation.

Background

Livestrong.com experienced explosive growth between 2015 and 2018. In that time, the content and product strategy struggled to keep up leading to disarray.

At the start of 2019, the Livestrong.com team came to consensus on a strategy: focus and enhance. Phase 1 involved the focusing and consolidation of our content library to 4 categories: Get Fit, Eat Better, Manage Your Weight, and Live Well. Phase 2 involved us improving the content and user experiences of within these categories.

This case study picks up at the beginning of 2020, when we began the enhancement phase of the Get Fit category - starting with workouts.

Research

Activities included: stakeholder and user interviews, competitor usability and feature analysis, schema and model research, and a look into user search patterns.

You can view a redacted findings and recommendations writeup here, but for brevity the key insights are:

Insight 1

People of beginner to intermediate fitness proficiency are more likely to search for exercises by body region (arms, chest, legs, etc.) over particular muscles or equipment.

Insight 2

People heavily prefer video that is laser focused on providing an efficient visual walkthrough of the movement with voiceover for additional clarification or detail.

Insight 3

Our competitors widely categorized their content by body region (in alignment with Insight 1) or by goal (build muscle, lose fat, etc.).

Insight 4

Our online competitors largely offered exercise instructions in the form of editorial: a visual asset (video, gif, or image) paired with a paragraph of explanation; while our native app competitors offered complete routines made up of individual exercises that leveraged structured data.

Insight 5

Schema.org had two schemas that we could leverage PhysicalActivity which mapped to an activity like "weight lifting" and ExercisePlan which mapped to an individual exercise like a dumbbell curl..

Recommendations

I synthesized my findings into a Recommendations section of my write-up. You can read it in detail below, but the jist of it is: roughly model our content organization to the two schemas found on schema.org, create companion front-end and CMS-side modules, along with a recommendation that I'm not at liberty to share.

I presented my research and recommendations along with some rough sketches to the higher ups and we agreed to a phased approach.

Case Study Midpoint Recap

Problem: Livestrong.com needs an improved workout content experience that helps people of beginner-to-intermediate fitness levels learn about the right ways to perform and execute individual exercises and comprehensive routines.

Solution: Build a structured data driven experience. The project is phased and starts off with our in-article exercise experience.

Important Note: From this point forward, the wires, research, and interations detailed below were developed in office, as well as, in class as part of my HCI studies. Parts of the work have made their way to production while other parts were created to serve as submissions for finals.
